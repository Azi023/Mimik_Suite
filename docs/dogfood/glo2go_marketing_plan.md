# Glo2Go Aesthetics — Marketing Plan & Content Pillars (2026 refresh)

> Drafted from the LIVE site (https://glo2goaesthetics.co.uk/) + current socials — the
> source of truth. The 2025 Drive brief/creatives are style history only: the brand has
> since added Polynucleotides and fat-dissolving (Aqualyx / Lemon Bottle), moved to
> "luxury, expertise, and affordability" positioning, and operates from 36 St Mary at
> Hill, London EC3R 8DU. **Operator review required before anything ships.**

## 1. Positioning (current, evidenced)

- **Line the site leads with:** "Your journey to beauty and wellness starts here … we
  combine **luxury, expertise, and affordability**."
- **Hero voice:** "REFINE. RESTORE. SUPPORT." — "Non-surgical treatments are created to
  enhance your natural features, **never overpower them**."
- **The 2026 story vs 2025:** the old brief sold "personalized, results-driven care";
  the live brand now adds an explicit **affordability** wedge against Harley-St pricing
  while keeping the premium register. Content should own that triangle: luxury feel,
  clinical expertise, honest prices.

## 2. Audience

London professionals (25–55) wanting natural-looking, non-surgical results from
credentialed practitioners; skews to City workers (EC3R location = lunchtime/after-work
appointments); secondary: hair-regrowth seekers (PRP) and body-contouring (fat
dissolving) — both underserved by the old creative set.

## 3. Voice & guardrails

Professional yet approachable; confident and reassuring; premium-but-accessible.
- **Do:** natural-enhancement framing · credential/safety proof · "Book Consultation"
  CTA · UK spelling · specific treatment names.
- **Don't:** perfection/overpromise claims · flaw-shaming ("fix your ...") · bargain-bin
  discount tone (offers stay premium: "seasonal package", not "CHEAP") · medical claims
  beyond scope.

## 4. Visual system (from the live site + logo study)

Primary purple **#8C4F8D**, neutral gray **#6B6A6A**, white space, soft lavender washes;
G2G interlocked wordmark (usable as-is per vision study); clean clinical photography,
flat-graphic accents, minimal complexity. Past Drive creatives (purple-on-white, badge
logo, before/after + educational explainers) remain directionally right → ingest as
style anchor, but defer to current site on any conflict.

## 5. Content pillars (proposed — persist in-app, operator confirms)

| # | Pillar | What it carries | Example topics |
|---|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Treatment Education | Authority + demand capture | "Polynucleotides, explained", "PRP vs PDRN microneedling" |
| 2 | Proof & Transformations | Consented before/afters, testimonials, "500+ happy clients" | Glutathione glow journey |
| 3 | Expertise & Safety | Practitioner credentials, tech, what-to-expect | "Your first consult: what happens" |
| 4 | Glow & Wellness Lifestyle | Skin health, wellness therapies, self-care | "Winter skin barrier basics" |
| 5 | Offers & Seasonal Moments | Premium-toned promotions and packages | "New-year renewal package" |

Cadence: the app's calendar replaces the old content-planner sheet. Suggested starting
rhythm: 4 posts/week — 2× Education, 1× Proof, 1× rotating (Lifestyle/Offer), stories ad
hoc. Priority launch topics: **Polynucleotides** and **fat dissolving** (new to the
lineup, zero legacy creatives exist for them).

## 6. KPIs (first 90 days in-app)

Consult bookings attributed from socials · IG follower growth · save/share rate on
Education posts · approval turnaround (client-portal SLA) inside Mimik Suite.
